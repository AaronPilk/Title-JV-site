The premise, in one paragraph.

Every purchase transaction you originate creates title revenue at closing. That revenue is almost certainly going to an outside title company. A properly structured Affiliated Business Arrangement under RESPA Safe Harbor 8(c)(4) may allow qualified mortgage operators to participate in that revenue through ownership of a real title-company LLC — not through referral fees, splits, or any per-loan payment. The qualification review is how we evaluate whether your production, market, and structure make the JV a fit.

Why mortgage operators consider this

Participate in the economics of the closings you originate.

Title insurance premium is generated on every purchase loan you close. Today, that premium flows to whichever title company the borrower or settlement agent selects — not to your business. A title JV structures co-ownership of a real operating company so that, when your production runs through it (with proper RESPA disclosure), you participate in distributions tied to that company's profitability.

This is not a referral-fee arrangement, a marketing services agreement, or a kickback structure — all of which are prohibited under RESPA. It is ownership of an operating settlement-services company, which is the exception RESPA Safe Harbor 8(c)(4) specifically permits.

The qualification review evaluates your monthly purchase volume, market, loan officer count, and current title relationships. Submissions that appear to fit get a private 30–45 minute strategy call.

Compliance

RESPA-compliant by structure, not by interpretation.

Section 8(c)(4) of the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act permits an Affiliated Business Arrangement under three conditions. The JV is built around all three from inception.

  1. 1

    THE PARTY MAKING THE REFERRAL MUST DISCLOSE THE ARRANGEMENT TO THE PERSON BEING REFERRED

  2. 2

    THE PERSON IS NOT REQUIRED TO USE ANY PARTICULAR PROVIDER OF SETTLEMENT SERVICES

  3. 3

    PERSON MAKING THE REFERRAL CAN’T RECEIVE ANY “THING OF VALUE” OTHER THAN A RETURN ON OWNERSHIP INTEREST
