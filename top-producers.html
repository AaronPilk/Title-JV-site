An honest qualification — the JV is not for every agent.

A title joint venture is structured for operators with consistent transaction volume and the ability to direct title routing with proper RESPA disclosure. For most agents the structure does not apply. For top producers — the agents whose monthly volume rivals a small team's — it may. The qualification review is how we evaluate whether your production, market, and the way your business is structured make the JV a fit.

Why high producers consider this

Participate in the economics of the closings you are already creating.

Title insurance premium is generated on every transaction you close. For an agent producing thirty, forty, fifty sides a year, that is significant ancillary revenue — and today it flows entirely to whichever title company the buyer, lender, or settlement agent selects. A title JV co-owns the operating company so that when your closings run through it (with proper RESPA disclosure to the consumer), you participate in distributions tied to the company's profitability.

This is not a per-transaction payment, a marketing services agreement, or any form of referral fee. Those are prohibited under RESPA. The Safe Harbor permits ownership in an operating settlement-services company — which is the structure of the JV.

Submissions are reviewed privately. If your production and market appear to fit, we will reach out to schedule a private 30–45 minute strategy call to walk through what the JV would look like for your specific business.

  • Co-ownership of a real operating title LLC
  • Distributions tied to company profitability, not referrals
  • RESPA Safe Harbor 8(c)(4) compliant structure
  • Department of Insurance licensing handled
  • National underwriter contracts in place
  • Escrow, technology, and licensed staff operated for you
  • Compliant disclosure templates and SOPs provided
  • Minimal ongoing operational burden on the agent

Market

Title routing

Whether you currently have meaningful influence over which title company handles your closings.

Compliance

RESPA-compliant by structure, not by interpretation.

RESPA Section 8(c)(4) permits an Affiliated Business Arrangement under three conditions. The JV is built around all three from inception.

  1. 1

    THE PARTY MAKING THE REFERRAL MUST DISCLOSE THE ARRANGEMENT TO THE PERSON BEING REFERRED

  2. 2

    THE PERSON IS NOT REQUIRED TO USE ANY PARTICULAR PROVIDER OF SETTLEMENT SERVICES

  3. 3

    PERSON MAKING THE REFERRAL CAN’T RECEIVE ANY “THING OF VALUE” OTHER THAN A RETURN ON OWNERSHIP INTEREST
