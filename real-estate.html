Real Estate Teams: Own More Of The Transaction Revenue You Help Create.

Real estate teams create transaction volume every month. The closing process generates title revenue, but most teams never participate in that ancillary revenue. A properly structured title JV may help qualified teams build additional revenue and long-term enterprise value from business they are already producing.

The premise, in one paragraph.

Every closing your team participates in creates title revenue. Today that revenue almost certainly flows to whichever title company the buyer, lender, or settlement agent chose — not to your brokerage or team. A properly structured Affiliated Business Arrangement under RESPA Safe Harbor 8(c)(4) may allow qualified real estate operators to participate in that revenue through ownership of a real title-company LLC. Not through referral fees. Not through marketing payments. Through ownership distributions tied to a real operating business.

Why brokers and team leaders consider this

Build enterprise value alongside transaction revenue.

A real estate team is a production engine. Title insurance premium is generated on every transaction that engine produces — and today, none of that premium routes back to the operators creating the volume. A title JV co-owns the operating company so that when your transaction flow runs through it (with proper RESPA disclosure to the consumer), the team participates in distributions based on the company's profitability.

The structural distinction matters: this is not a referral-fee arrangement, a marketing services agreement, or any form of per-transaction payment. Those are prohibited under RESPA. The Safe Harbor permits ownership of an operating settlement-services company — which is what the JV is.

For larger teams and brokerages, the structure also creates a durable asset: an operating LLC with revenue, distributions, and enterprise value that exists outside of any individual agent's production.

  • Co-ownership of a real operating title LLC
  • Distributions tied to company profitability, not referrals
  • RESPA Safe Harbor 8(c)(4) compliant structure
  • Enterprise value separate from individual agent production
  • Department of Insurance licensing handled
  • National underwriter contracts in place
  • Escrow, technology, and licensed staff operated for you
  • Compliant disclosure templates and SOPs provided

How qualification works for real estate teams.

The review focuses on four areas. There is no fixed minimum, but consistent annual volume and clarity around team structure matter more than peak months.

Annual transactions

Total closed sides across the last 12 months, with emphasis on consistency.

Market

We currently operate in NC and SC, with adjacent markets evaluated case-by-case.

Team structure

Number of agents and whether you are the broker, owner, or decision-maker for title routing.

Current title relationship

Whether your team currently routes through a preferred company, multiple companies, or the client/lender chooses.

Compliance

RESPA-compliant by structure, not by interpretation.

RESPA Section 8(c)(4) permits an Affiliated Business Arrangement under three conditions. The JV is built around all three from inception.

  1. 1

    THE PARTY MAKING THE REFERRAL MUST DISCLOSE THE ARRANGEMENT TO THE PERSON BEING REFERRED

  2. 2

    THE PERSON IS NOT REQUIRED TO USE ANY PARTICULAR PROVIDER OF SETTLEMENT SERVICES

  3. 3

    PERSON MAKING THE REFERRAL CAN’T RECEIVE ANY “THING OF VALUE” OTHER THAN A RETURN ON OWNERSHIP INTEREST
